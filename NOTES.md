## 2026-06-14 (session 3 — Watch shortcut sync diagnosis)
- Done: Long diagnostic on why the voice-bridge iOS Shortcut won't appear on the Apple Watch. Ruled out, in order: "Show on Apple Watch" toggle (on), unsupported actions (3× trivial dummy shortcuts also fail to appear), iCloud Drive (on), Watch storage (plenty), Apple ID mismatch (same account on iPhone/Watch/iCloud.com/Macs), and Watch-side corruption (full unpair + re-pair *with restore* didn't help). Key finding: **iCloud Notes sync perfectly across all 4 devices** → account/CloudKit is healthy → the problem is **isolated to the Shortcuts CloudKit sync zone**, which is wedged in a "deleted" tombstone state ("iCloud Shortcuts Deleted — Continue Syncing?" dialog recurs on the Mac). No device can push shortcuts to iCloud — edits, a duplicate, and a fresh **Mac-origin** shortcut all fail to propagate to iPhone/Watch. Not a beta OS; Developer Mode and the Tailscale VPN profile both ruled out as causes. Likely trigger: iCloud Drive Shortcuts data got deleted at some point (toggling iCloud Drive off wipes it).
- Decided: Root cause = wedged/corrupted **iCloud Shortcuts CloudKit zone**, Shortcuts-specific. The Watch can ONLY receive shortcuts via iCloud sync, so it stays blocked until the zone heals — re-pairing/toggling cannot fix it. The iPhone shortcut itself works fine; only the Watch trigger is on hold. Voice shortcut backed up as a shared iCloud link before touching sync.
- Next: (1) Click **Continue Syncing on the iPhone** and leave overnight on Wi-Fi — the "recently deleted" tombstone often self-clears within ~a day. (2) If still broken: **iCloud sign-out/in on the iPhone** to rebuild CloudKit zones — currently blocked by a "Sign out unavailable due to restrictions" message; re-verify Screen Time → Content & Privacy Restrictions → Account Changes = Allow. (3) Else **Apple Support** to reset the iCloud Shortcuts zone server-side, and file Feedback Assistant.

## 2026-06-14 (session 4 cont. — Pushcut PRO assessment + Mac-mini auto-speak path)
- Done: Assessed paying for Pushcut PRO to get zero-tap auto-speak. Probed the mini for a Pushcut-free alternative: `say` is present (`/usr/bin/say`), default output is built-in **Mac mini Speakers**, and a 1080p display/GUI session is attached. Since voice-bridge already runs on the mini, on completion it could just call `say "<result>"` — no Pushcut, no PRO, no extra device, no tap.
- Decided: **Do NOT buy Pushcut PRO** for auto-speak. PRO unlocks dynamic content (minor) + the Automation Server, but reliable no-tap execution needs a *dedicated always-on iOS device* we don't have; on a daily-driver iPhone iOS suspends it. The free tap-to-hear setup is near the practical ceiling for a single phone. Better direction: use the always-on Mac mini (or the active Mac) to speak via `say`.
- Next: **Investigate Mac-mini `say` auto-speak.** Open questions: (1) does `say` actually emit audio from the **tmux-over-SSH service context** (audio routing from a non-foreground/SSH session is often blocked — test before building); (2) **earshot** — if the owner sits at the iMac and the mini is elsewhere, route speech to the *active* Mac instead (voice-bridge SSHes to the iMac to `say`, or a tiny listener on the iMac). Decide target device, then wire completion → `say` as an alternative/addition to the Pushcut doorbell.
